Clinical Guidelines Quick Reference
Short reference for staging, treatment planning, monitoring, and protocol comparison during the demo.
High Risk / LA
India practice checkpoints for high-risk localized or locally advanced disease
▾
Core Workup
Confirm biopsy grade, PSA history, DRE/TNM staging, and baseline performance status.
Use PSMA PET-CT where available; if unavailable, complete bone scan/SPECT and cross-sectional imaging.
Discuss every high-risk case in MDT before definitive RT or systemic intensification.
Treatment Pattern
Definitive RT with long-course ADT remains the backbone for cT3/cN1 non-metastatic disease.
Bone health assessment should be bundled with ADT start: DEXA, calcium/Vit D, and bone protection where indicated.
Escalate with ARSI when clinically appropriate and financially feasible after MDT review.
Use this tab to explain practical India workflow constraints: imaging access, government coverage approvals, and formulary-driven treatment sequencing.
ADT Monitoring
Minimum monitoring expectations once treatment starts
▾
PSA every 3 months for the first 2 years, then 6-monthly if clinically stable.
Check testosterone when PSA response is inadequate or castration status is uncertain.
Repeat bone health review on long-term ADT; do not leave DEXA and bone protection untracked.
NCCN 2024
Key NCCN framing for high-risk and metastatic hormone-sensitive disease
▾
PSMA PET is preferred for initial staging in unfavorable intermediate, high, very high, and recurrent disease when available.
Treatment intensification with ARSI is expected more often in high-risk HSPC and mHSPC than in many India pathways.
Genomic testing thresholds are broader, especially when family history or advanced disease raises PARP eligibility questions.
Response
Outcome and surveillance emphasis
▾
Document nadir, biochemical recurrence triggers, and time-to-treatment milestones consistently.
Escalate imaging or systemic review early when PSA response diverges from expected treatment trajectory.
Staging
India vs NCCN
Both frameworks increasingly favor PSMA PET for high-risk staging.
India workflows still often rely on CT + bone scan because of access or payer delays.
Shared direction: better molecular staging. Operational difference: how often it is realistically completed before treatment start.
Systemic Therapy
Escalation intensity
NCCN-normalized practice intensifies earlier and more consistently with ARSI.
India adoption is often limited by coverage, out-of-pocket cost, and formulary barriers.
Demo point: the dashboard’s ARSI gap is clinically meaningful, not just an administrative artifact.
Low / Intermediate
Lower-risk disease
▾
Localized disease with lower PSA/Gleason burden may be managed with surveillance or localized treatment pathways.
High / Very High
High-risk, cT3+, Gleason ≥8, PSA >20
▾
Needs complete staging before definitive treatment and should be reviewed in MDT.
Flag missing PSMA PET, bone scan, DEXA, testosterone monitoring, and genetic testing where indicated.
M1 / Advanced
Metastatic presentation
▾
Prioritize systemic therapy selection, supportive care, and documentation of response milestones.
